# Storybook Watercolor

A soft hand-painted illustration style with whimsical charm. This design system evokes the feeling of a beloved children's storybook brought to life through delicate watercolor washes, visible brushstrokes, and dreamy atmospheric quality.

---

## 1. Visual Theme

- **Philosophy**: Nostalgic, gentle, and emotionally resonant. Every element should feel touched by a human hand rather than generated by a machine.
- **Mood**: Dreamy, warm, inviting, slightly imperfect in an endearing way.
- **Aesthetic**: Traditional watercolor painting on textured paper. Soft edges, organic shapes, and visible artistic process.
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Visible brushstrokes and pigment pooling
  - Soft color bleeds where hues gently merge
  - White space used intentionally as a design element
  - Delicate ink or pencil line work layered over washes
  - Natural, organic silhouettes — nothing perfectly geometric
  - Atmospheric, slightly faded quality as if aged by sunlight

---

| Role | Color Name | Hex | Usage |

| Primary | Sage Green | #8FAE8F | Nature elements, foliage, secondary washes |
| Primary | Warm Earth | #C4A882 | Ground, tree trunks, warm shadows |
| Background | Watercolor Paper | #F7F3EB | Primary canvas, textured paper base |
| Background | Cream | #FDFBF7 | Light sections, breathing room |
| Accent | Deep Indigo | #3D5A6C | Emphasis, deeper pigment pools, shadows |
| Accent | Rose Madder | #D4847C | Warm highlights, flowers, gentle warnings |
| Accent | Honey Gold | #D4A843 | Sunlight, magical elements, stars |
| Accent | Lavender Gray | #A89FB8 | Distant elements, mist, subtle depth |
| Neutral | Ink Brown | #5C4A3A | Line work, text, fine details |

**Color Usage Rules**:
- All colors should appear as translucent washes rather than solid fills
- Layer multiple light washes to build depth rather than using a single dark value
- Allow colors to bleed softly into one another at boundaries
- Reserve pure white (#FFFFFF) only for paper highlights and sparkle effects

## 3. Typography

| Role | Font Style | Weight | Size | Usage |
|------|-----------|--------|------|-------|
| Display | Elegant hand-lettering or script | Regular | 48-72px | Page titles, storybook chapter headings |
| Heading | Rounded serif or soft sans | Medium | 32-40px | Section headers, scene titles |
| Body | Friendly serif or humanist sans | Regular | 16-20px | Paragraph text, descriptions |
| Caption | Delicate script or light serif | Light | 12-14px | Annotations, whispered asides, image captions |
| Accent | Hand-drawn inline | Regular | 18-24px | Pull quotes, magical words |

**Typography Rules**:
- Prefer fonts with slight irregularities and organic curves
- Text should feel integrated with illustrations, not placed on top of them
- Use generous line-height (1.6-1.8) to maintain airy, breathable text blocks
- Subtle text-shadow or offset in ink brown can simulate hand-lettered depth
- Avoid sharp, geometric sans-serifs; avoid monospace entirely

---

## 4. Components

### Buttons
- Shape: Soft rounded rectangles or organic pill shapes with slightly uneven edges
- Fill: Light watercolor wash in primary color, 60-70% opacity
- Border: None, or a delicate ink line that appears hand-drawn
- Hover: Slightly deeper pigment pool, gentle color bloom outward
- Shadow: None — use soft color bleeds instead of drop shadows

### Cards
- Background: Cream or watercolor paper texture with subtle grain
- Border: None, or a faint hand-drawn ink rectangle
- Corner: Slightly rounded (8-12px) with organic imperfection
- Content: Generous internal padding (24-32px), illustration-integrated

### Icons
- Style: Hand-drawn line icons with variable stroke width
- Stroke: Ink brown or deep indigo, 1.5-2px
- Fill: Optional light wash behind line work
- Character: Slightly wobbly, imperfect circles and curves

### Inputs
- Background: Transparent or very light wash
- Border: Bottom-only ink line or full hand-drawn rectangle
- Focus: Gentle color bloom in primary blue or sage green
- Placeholder: Light gray, italic, as if lightly penciled in

---

## 5. Layout

- **Approach**: Asymmetrical and organic. Avoid rigid grids.
- **Spacing**: Generous, breathing room between elements. White space is as important as content.
- **Composition**: Elements may overlap gently, as if painted in layers on the same page.
- **Containers**: Soft, undefined edges. Content areas may fade into the paper background.
- **Rule of Thumb**: If a layout feels too structured, break it slightly — offset an image, let a wash extend beyond its frame, tilt a text block by 1-2 degrees.

---

## 6. Depth

- **Method**: Layered translucent washes rather than geometric shadows
- **Shadows**: Soft, diffused, colored shadows (e.g., a warm earth shadow under a sage green element)
- **Highlight**: Reserved white paper showing through, or a light honey gold touch
- **Layering**: Build depth by stacking 2-4 translucent color layers
- **Atmospheric Perspective**: Distant elements are lighter, more lavender-gray, and less detailed
- **No**: Sharp drop shadows, solid black shadows, bevel/emboss effects

---

## 7. Do / Don'ts

### Do
- Use visible brushstroke textures and soft color bleeds
- Embrace white space as an active design element
- Layer delicate line work over color washes
- Use organic, natural shapes with gentle curves
- Create dreamy, atmospheric backgrounds with fading edges
- Integrate typography with illustrations
- Allow imperfections and happy accidents

### Don't
- Use perfect geometric shapes or rigid grids
- Apply solid, flat fills without translucency variation
- Add digital-looking gradients or glossy effects
- Use sharp, angular corners on containers
- Create photorealistic elements
- Use neon, metallic, or high-tech visual effects
- Crowd the canvas — let elements breathe

---

## 8. Responsive

- **Mobile**: Maintain generous spacing even on small screens; stack elements vertically with soft separating washes
- **Tablet**: Allow illustrations to break out of column boundaries slightly
- **Desktop**: Use asymmetrical two-column layouts with overlapping illustration and text areas
- **Images**: Watercolor illustrations should remain full-bleed or have soft, irregular edges
- **Touch**: Buttons should have large, soft tap targets with gentle visual feedback

---

## 9. Agent Prompt Guide

When generating UI with this style, instruct the AI to:

1. Apply a watercolor paper texture as the base background
2. Use soft, muted color washes in dusty blue, sage green, and warm earth tones
3. Add visible brushstroke textures to all colored elements
4. Create soft color bleeds at boundaries instead of hard edges
5. Use delicate ink-brown line work for text and fine details
6. Layer translucent washes to build depth rather than using solid shadows
7. Keep shapes organic and natural — avoid perfect geometry
8. Integrate typography as if hand-lettered onto the illustration
9. Preserve generous white space and allow elements to breathe
10. Add subtle atmospheric effects: fading edges, distant mist, gentle pigment pooling

**Keywords to include**: watercolor, hand-painted, storybook, whimsical, soft washes, visible brushstrokes, organic shapes, dreamy atmosphere, textured paper, delicate line work

**Avoid**: geometric, flat design, neon, glossy, photorealistic, sharp angles, solid fills, digital gradients
