# IG Chalkboard

A classic classroom chalkboard aesthetic with hand-drawn chalk illustrations. This design system captures the nostalgic warmth of traditional teaching — imperfect, sketchy lines, colorful chalk accents, and the authentic texture of a well-used blackboard.

---

## 1. Visual Theme

- **Philosophy**: Authentic, approachable, and warmly imperfect. The charm lies in the human hand behind every mark.
- **Mood**: Nostalgic, educational, playful, informal.
- **Aesthetic**: A real chalkboard that has been written on, erased, and written on again. Dust particles, faint smudges, and the texture of chalk on slate.
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Hand-drawn chalk illustrations with sketchy, imperfect lines
  - Chalk dust effects around text and key elements
  - Playful doodles: stars, arrows, underlines, circles, checkmarks
  - Mathematical formulas and simple diagrams scattered as decoration
  - Eraser smudges and chalk residue textures
  - Optional wooden frame border for extra classroom authenticity
  - Stick figures and simple icons drawn in moments of inspiration

---

## 2. Color Palette

| Role | Color Name | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----------|-----|-------|
| Background | Chalkboard Black | #1A1A1A | Primary background, main board |
| Alt Background | Green-Black | #1C2B1C | Traditional green chalkboard variant |
| Primary Text | Chalk White | #F5F5F5 | Main text, outlines, primary content |
| Accent 1 | Chalk Yellow | #FFE566 | Highlights, emphasis, stars, key terms |
| Accent 2 | Chalk Pink | #FF9999 | Secondary highlights, gentle warnings, hearts |
| Accent 3 | Chalk Blue | #66B3FF | Diagrams, links, cool emphasis |
| Accent 4 | Chalk Green | #90EE90 | Success states, nature, checkmarks |
| Accent 5 | Chalk Orange | #FFB366 | Warnings, energy, warm emphasis |
| Texture | Chalk Dust | rgba(245,245,245,0.15) | Smudge effects, erased areas |
| Frame | Wood Brown | #8B5E3C | Optional border, shelf accents |

**Color Usage Rules**:
- Background should always be dark — either black or deep green-black
- Text and elements should look like chalk: slightly dusty, imperfect edges, variable opacity
- Use accent colors sparingly to create visual hierarchy
- White chalk is the default; colored chalk is for emphasis only

---

## 3. Typography

| Role | Font Style | Weight | Size | Usage |
|------|-----------|--------|------|-------|
| Display | Hand-drawn chalk lettering | Bold | 48-64px | Main titles, lesson headers |
| Heading | Chalk script or informal sans | Medium | 28-36px | Section headers, topic names |
| Body | Chalk-style sans or handwriting | Regular | 16-20px | Explanations, body text |
| Caption | Small chalk handwriting | Light | 12-14px | Annotations, side notes, doodles |
| Accent | Decorative chalk symbols | Regular | 20-32px | Arrows, stars, emphasis marks |

**Typography Rules**:
- All text should have a slight chalk texture or dust effect
- Baseline should be subtly imperfect — letters may drift up or down by 1-2px
- Use white chalk for 80% of text; reserve colors for emphasis
- Allow "chalk residue" — faint ghost letters where something was erased
- Line-height: 1.5-1.7 to simulate natural handwriting spacing

## 4. Components

### Buttons
- Shape: Hand-drawn rectangles or rounded shapes with slightly wobbly edges
- Fill: Transparent or faint chalk dust background
- Border: 2-3px chalk-white line, imperfectly drawn
- Text: Chalk white or appropriate accent color
- Hover: Slight chalk dust accumulation, or a smudge effect around the edges
- Active: A small eraser-smudge overlay

### Cards
- Background: Transparent or very faint chalk dust rectangle
- Border: Hand-drawn chalk rectangle, possibly with corner doodles
- Corner: Slightly rounded by hand, not perfectly circular
- Shadow: None — use chalk dust smudges for depth instead

### Icons
- Style: Simple stick figures, hand-drawn symbols, basic geometric doodles
- Stroke: Chalk white or accent color, 2-3px with slight texture
- Character: Imperfect circles, wobbly arrows, sketchy stars
- Examples: Checkmarks with a flourish, arrows with little wings, stars with uneven points

### Inputs
- Background: Transparent
- Border: Bottom chalk line or full hand-drawn rectangle
- Focus: A soft chalk dust glow or underline in accent color
- Placeholder: Faint chalk gray, as if lightly sketched and half-erased

---

## 5. Layout

- **Approach**: Informal and free-flowing, like a teacher's spontaneous board layout.
- **Spacing**: Moderate to generous, with elements arranged in loose zones rather than strict columns.
- **Composition**: Content blocks may be tilted slightly (0.5-1 degree) for authenticity.
- **Zones**: Divide the board into informal sections using hand-drawn dividing lines or chalk dashes.
- **Decorative**: Scatter small doodles, formulas, and annotations in margins and empty spaces.

---

## 6. Depth

- **Method**: Chalk dust layering and smudge effects
- **Shadows**: None in the traditional sense — use faint chalk residue and dust clouds instead
- **Highlight**: Brighter chalk strokes on top of darker board texture
- **Layering**: Multiple light chalk layers create depth; erased areas are lighter
- **Texture**: The board itself provides depth through its grain and scratch marks
- **No**: Drop shadows, glows, bevels, or any digital depth effects

---

## 7. Do / Don'ts

### Do
- Maintain authentic chalk texture on all elements
- Use imperfect, hand-drawn quality throughout
- Add subtle chalk dust and smudge effects around active elements
- Create visual hierarchy with color variety (white + 4 accent chalks)
- Include playful doodles and annotations in margins
- Use sketchy, informal connection lines between related elements
- Allow elements to look like they were drawn in real time

### Don't
- Use perfect geometric shapes or clean digital-looking lines
- Create photorealistic elements or photographs
- Apply gradients, glossy effects, or drop shadows
- Use thin, precise strokes — chalk is thick and slightly dusty
- Make the board look brand new — embrace wear and residue
- Use light backgrounds — this style requires a dark board

---

## 8. Responsive

- **Mobile**: Single-column layout with stacked chalk-drawn sections; maintain dark background
- **Tablet**: Two informal columns separated by a hand-drawn chalk line
- **Desktop**: Spacious board layout with zones for navigation, content, and decorative doodles
- **Touch**: Large tap targets with chalk-dust feedback on press
- **Images**: Any images should be rendered as chalk sketches or have a chalk-filter overlay

---

## 9. Agent Prompt Guide

When generating UI with this style, instruct the AI to:

1. Set the background to deep chalkboard black (#1A1A1A) or green-black (#1C2B1C)
2. Apply a realistic chalkboard texture with subtle scratches and grain
3. Use chalk-white (#F5F5F5) as the primary text and element color
4. Add chalk dust and smudge effects around all interactive elements
5. Draw all shapes with imperfect, hand-sketched lines (2-3px stroke)
6. Use colored chalk accents (yellow, pink, blue, green, orange) sparingly for emphasis
7. Include playful doodles: stars, arrows, underlines, stick figures, simple diagrams
8. Add faint eraser marks and chalk residue in empty areas
9. Make typography informal with slightly irregular baselines
10. Optional: Add a wooden frame border for classroom authenticity

**Keywords to include**: chalkboard, hand-drawn, chalk texture, classroom, educational, sketchy lines, chalk dust, nostalgic, informal, doodles, smudges

**Avoid**: clean lines, geometric precision, gradients, glossy effects, photorealistic, light backgrounds, perfect symmetry, digital crispness
