# Teenage Skate Scribble Style

## Overview
An edgy, rebellious street art aesthetic inspired by skate culture, graffiti, and teenage angst. Features raw, energetic mark-making with a DIY punk attitude. Ideal for youth-oriented brands, music content, and counter-culture messaging.

## Visual Characteristics
- **Line Art**: Rough, scratchy, overlapping strokes; some lines appear hastily drawn
- **Color Palette**: Muted earth tones mixed with neon accents (safety orange, electric green)
- **Texture**: Gritty, grainy overlays; spray paint drips; paper distress
- **Typography**: Hand-scrawled, uneven lettering; mix of uppercase and lowercase; some words circled or underlined
- **Iconography**: Skateboards, band logos, anarchist symbols, torn stickers
- **Background**: Concrete gray, brick wall texture, or notebook paper with coffee stains

## Composition Rules
- Layered, chaotic arrangement with elements overlapping intentionally
- Diagonal energy lines and arrows creating movement
- Sticker-like elements appearing to be slapped on randomly
- Handwritten annotations and corrections (crossed-out words, arrows)
- Asymmetrical with strong visual weight on one side

## Prompt Template
```
Create a teenage skate scribble poster featuring {SUBJECT}.

Style: Raw street art with gritty textures, spray paint effects, and DIY punk aesthetics.

Subject: {SUBJECT_ACTION} in a dynamic, rebellious pose.

Typography: Hand-scrawled text "{MAIN_TEXT}" with uneven lettering, some words circled or crossed out. Add "{SECONDARY_TEXT}" as a smaller annotation.

Textures: Apply grainy overlays, spray paint drips, and paper distress effects.

Decorative: Include skate stickers, band logos, torn tape, and anarchist symbols scattered around.

Background: Concrete wall or distressed notebook paper with coffee stains and wrinkles.

Mood: Rebellious, energetic, raw, unpolished, authentic.
```

## Variable Substitutions
- `{SUBJECT}`: Main character or object
- `{SUBJECT_ACTION}`: What the subject is doing (skating, playing guitar, etc.)
- `{MAIN_TEXT}`: Bold headline
- `{SECONDARY_TEXT}`: Subheading or annotation
- `{LOCATION}`: Urban setting (skate park, alley, basement)

## Example Usage
```
SUBJECT: a teenage skater
SUBJECT_ACTION: doing a kickflip
MAIN_TEXT: "NO RULES"
SECONDARY_TEXT: "just ride"
LOCATION: concrete skate park
```

## Aspect Ratio Adaptations
- **1:1 (Square)**: Tight crop, layered stickers and text, chaotic energy
- **16:9 (Landscape)**: Wide scene with subject in action, urban backdrop
- **9:16 (Portrait)**: Vertical poster format, stacked elements, phone wallpaper style
- **4:3 (Standard)**: Zine page layout with margins and annotations
